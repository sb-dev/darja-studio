Produced a complete Chapter 2 draft for a central urban/Algiers-oriented beginner baseline. The chapter teaches a doorstep interaction engine: `s-slaam ʿlikom` and its response, `waash raak/raaki/raakom`, `la baas`, `l-ḥamdullah`, returned `u nta/nti/ntuma`, `marḥba`, `marḥba bik/bikom`, `tfaḍḍal/tfaḍḍli/tfaḍḍlu`, and `b-slaama`. It covers individual, gendered singular and group address, brief wellbeing exchanges, welcome, warm repetition, entering a home and leave-taking.

Recognition material includes morning greetings, eastern `waash aḥwaalek` and unresolved `ʿaslaama`, western-documented respectful kin/pilgrimage titles, and French greeting switches. These are explicitly bounded and not promoted to productive use. The older-person guidance recommends greeting and patient interaction rather than guessing a title or inventing a French-style plural honorific rule.

Included three specified sentence frames, accessible and ordinary-speed dialogues, trilingual line-by-line text, pronunciation work on rhythm groups, `ḥ`, gemination and consonant clusters, pragmatics, regional notes, rooted conversational context, retrieval/substitution/listening work, an information-gap doorway task, personalised recording work, quick review and recurrence scheduling.
