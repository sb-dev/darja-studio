Three sentence frames handle non-understanding plus repetition, lexical meaning/wording questions, and polite correction/reformulation. An accessible dialogue corrects “Sami” to “Sam”; an ordinary-speed dialogue adds a purposeful French `Tu parles français ?` switch, a Darja meaning request and a Darja-language preference. Pronunciation work targets the `ma…-sh` rhythm frame, the `fh` cluster, `ʿaawed`, and non-confrontational correction stress.

The rooted context treats translation, repetition, reformulation and code-switching as repertoire management without assuming French is universal or assigning every switch one historical or social motive. Colonial/postcolonial language hierarchy is mentioned only through documented policy and multilingual-practice mechanisms.
